#Orchid Collection Exercise

###Description: 
I have been collecting orchids since ~2012, but have not always been good about keeping notes on when I got new plants, or when I killed plants. After ~2021, I started taking better notes. To work on learning Python, I took it upon myself to analyze some of the trends and patterns hidden within my collection log.

###Background:
Orchid collectors are a peculiar bunch. Every one of them openly acknowlegdes that collecting orchids is addicting, and the general advice is quite enabeling to that "addiction." Have you tried growing that species several times now, and kill it each time? Well, why not try it again? Further, "There's always room for one more..." is a mantra familiar with all orchid hobbyists. Perhaps the strangest saying among growers is "One is really an expert grower until they've killed 10,000 plants." The spirit of the phrase is clear - mortality happens and each dead plant in your collection represents how you have learned. However, if I was eager to become an expert grower, I could accomplish the same benchmark by commmitting arson at a orchid nursery. Nevertheless, the idea of becoming an expert after killing 10,000 plants raises some interesting questions.

###Question 1
Am I actually getting better at growing orchids? If so, I would hypothesize that the rate of orchid mortality in my collection would be declining over time.
